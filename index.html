The Math Recovery Project

Slowly Reversing the Impact of COVID-19 on Math

Our Goal

The pandemic left a massive gap in student learning, causing global mathematics scores to plunge by an average of 14 percent of a standard deviation. We saw this crisis firsthand in our own hallways when we discovered that a little over ⅓ of our school's middle schoolers were failing pre-algebra. To fight back, I joined forces with three peers to launch a targeted student tutoring program and this project is our mission to scale that support, close the COVID learning gap, and give every student the exact tools they need to master math with confidence.

What you can do!

Whether you're a student, parent, or educator, there are many ways you can help close the COVID learning gap in math. Here are some resources to get you started:

  • Math Motivators: a place to find math resources for students and educators. Along with a place locally to volunteer.
  • Khan Academy: a free online resource with lessons and practice exercises for all levels of math.
  • Idealist Volunteer Match : A platform that helps you find volunteer opportunities in your community to help reduce the learning gap.
  • Upchieve: a free tutoring service that connects students with volunteer tutors.

Furthermore, you can also call your local school district to inquire about available math support programs or voluteer to help supprt struggling students.

Every effort counts in helping to close the COVID learning gap in math and ensuring that all students have the opportunity to succeed!
